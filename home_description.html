JiM is a project financed by the Driving Urban Transitions Partnership (DUT), which has been co-funded by the European Commission. The project is aimed at addressing the justice and equity aspects within the 15-minute city planning concept.

It represents a collaborative effort involving universities and public stakeholders across several countries. In Sweden, partners include Malmö University, Lund University, the municipalities of Gothenburg and Malmö, the Swedish Knowledge Center for Public Transport (K2), and the Public Transport Authorities in Skåne and Västra Götaland. From Norway, the University College of Molde is involved. Poland is represented by the University of Gdansk, Cracow University of Technology, and the Municipality of Koszalin. France contributes with the Institut d’études politiques de Grenoble and the Agence d’urbanisme de la région Grenobloise. Spain's participant is the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona, while Turkey's contributors are Gazi University and the Municipality of Bodrum.

The goal of JiM is to generate knowledge and develop planning tools for equitable urban planning. This initiative focuses on examining people's actual mobility patterns and their access to essential services, while also critically evaluating the impact of urban planning on equitable access for city residents.

Employing a mixed-methods approach, the project integrates various methodologies to understand the needs and desires of the public concerning the spatial distribution of crucial services and amenities. It also scrutinizes the decisions made by both public and private service providers regarding their locations. The outcomes of JiM's efforts are expected to significantly contribute to creating a more equitable and sustainable urban environment for a majority of city dwellers.
